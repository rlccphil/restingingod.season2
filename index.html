Season 2 — Resting in God's Presence
Days 6–10  ·  A 10-Season Journey

"The Lord is near to all who call on Him." — Psalm 145:18

Learning to Be With God

Without Needing to Perform

In this season, the focus shifts from merely stopping activity to becoming more aware of God's presence. Many people know how to work for God, study about God, or serve God — but quietly being with God can feel unfamiliar. We may feel restless, distracted, or pressured to "do something spiritual." These Sabbath practices are invitations to slow down enough to notice that God is already near.

🌿

Move Slowly

Resist the urge to rush. Let your body and heart settle into a gentler pace throughout each Sabbath day.

👂

Listen Deeply

Pay attention to what surfaces in silence — thoughts, emotions, Scripture, and the quiet movements of the Spirit.

🤫

Welcome Silence

Silence is not emptiness. It is the space where we become aware of what has always been present: God's nearness.

☀️

Enjoy His Presence

The goal is not performance but attentiveness. Allow yourself to simply enjoy being with God.

How to Use These Guidelines

  • 1Before — Prepare your heart and environment for a slower, more attentive Sabbath before the day begins.
  • 2During — Practice simple rhythms throughout the day that help you become more aware of God's presence.
  • 3After — At day's end, reflect on what you noticed, experienced, or learned. Use the journal space provided.
  • 4Journal — Write your personal reflections freely. You can email your reflections to yourself or a trusted companion using the form in each day's section.
  • 5No pressure — Do not expect dramatic spiritual experiences. Simply show up and be attentive.

Day 6 of 10 — Season 2
Day6

Silence Before God

📖 Psalm 62:1–2
  • Begin reducing noise and stimulation before your Sabbath starts.
  • Put your phone on silent for extended periods if possible.
  • Prepare a quiet place where you can sit comfortably.
Opening Prayer

"Lord, help me become attentive to Your presence."

  • Read Psalm 62:1–2 slowly.
  • Sit in silence for 10–20 minutes. Do not try to force thoughts or emotions.
  • When your mind wanders, gently return your attention to God.
  • Repeat quietly: "Here I am, Lord."
  • Spend time outdoors listening to the sounds around you.
  • ?What emotions surfaced during silence?
  • ?What distractions were hardest to release?
  • Thank God for meeting you even in quietness.
  • End your Sabbath with slow, unhurried prayer.

Day 7 of 10 — Season 2
Day7

Abiding in Christ

📖 John 15:1–5
  • Read John 15:1–5 before your Sabbath begins.
  • Reflect on the difference between striving and abiding.
  • Release the pressure to be productive today.
Opening Prayer

"Jesus, teach me to remain in You."

  • Spend time reading portions of John 15 slowly.
  • Sit quietly and imagine yourself resting in Christ like a branch connected to a vine.
  • Avoid unnecessary busyness.
  • Whenever you feel pressured internally, pause and breathe deeply.
  • Take a slow walk and thank God for sustaining your life.
  • ?What helps me remain connected to Christ?
  • ?What pulls me away from abiding?
  • Write one sentence describing how you experienced God today.
  • Close with gratitude.

Day 8 of 10 — Season 2
Day8

Listening to God

📖 1 Samuel 3:1–10
  • Enter your Sabbath with a teachable posture.
  • Bring a notebook or journal with you.
  • Release the need for immediate answers.
Opening Prayer

"Speak, Lord, for Your servant is listening."

  • Read 1 Samuel 3:1–10 slowly.
  • Spend several moments throughout the day listening rather than speaking in prayer.
  • Ask gentle questions: "Lord, what do You want me to notice?" and "What is happening in my soul today?"
  • Pay attention to Scripture, emotions, thoughts, and moments of peace.
  • Do not force spiritual impressions.
  • ?What did I notice today?
  • ?Did I sense God inviting me toward anything?
  • Write down one meaningful insight or feeling from the day.
  • Thank God for His nearness.

Day 9 of 10 — Season 2
Day9

Enjoying God

📖 Psalm 16
  • Prepare your heart to receive joy as a spiritual gift.
  • Let go of the idea that spirituality must always feel serious or intense.
Opening Prayer

"Lord, help me delight in You today."

  • Read Psalm 16 slowly.
  • Spend time doing something that refreshes your soul: listening to music, sitting in nature, enjoying a meal, spending time with loved ones.
  • Notice moments of beauty and joy throughout the day.
  • Thank God for small gifts throughout the day.
  • Smile often and receive delight without guilt.
  • ?What brought joy to my soul today?
  • ?Was it difficult for me to receive delight?
  • Offer thanks to God for specific moments of goodness.
  • End with worship or quiet gratitude.

Day10

Waiting on God

📖 Isaiah 40:28–31
  • Release the pressure to "figure everything out."
  • Acknowledge any burdens, worries, or unanswered questions you are carrying.
Opening Prayer

"Lord, teach me to wait with trust."

  • Read Isaiah 40:28–31 slowly.
  • Spend time resting without trying to solve problems.
  • Resist the urge to constantly seek productivity or answers.
  • Sit quietly for several moments throughout the day.
  • When anxiety arises, breathe deeply and say: "My hope is in God."
  • Let your Sabbath become an act of trust rather than control.
  • ?What situations am I struggling to surrender?
  • ?What did waiting feel like today?
  • Ask God for renewed strength for the coming week.
  • Close your Sabbath with a prayer of trust and peace.
