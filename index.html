Season 1 — Learning to Stop

Entering God's Rest Through Letting Go of Hurry

"Be still, and know that I am God." — Psalm 46:10

🕊️

Welcome to Season 1

This first season is about learning to stop. Many of us live with constant movement in our minds, bodies, and schedules. These Sabbath guides are gentle invitations to slow down and practice the presence of God.

These guides are not meant to become another set of tasks to complete. Move slowly. Adapt freely. Receive grace. You do not need to perform for God today.

How Each Day Works

🌅

Before

Practices to prepare your heart, mind, body, and environment before your Sabbath begins.

☀️

During

Gentle invitations to practice rest, worship, delight, attentiveness, and peace throughout the day.

🌙

After

Reflections to help you carry the grace and peace of Sabbath back into your ordinary life.

1Day

Receiving Rest

Matthew 11:28–30
  • Finish only the most essential responsibilities before your Sabbath begins.
  • Put away work-related materials if possible.
  • Silence unnecessary notifications on your phone.
  • Take three slow breaths and become aware of God's presence.
Opening Prayer"Lord, I release my unfinished work to You."
  • Read Matthew 11:28–30 slowly.
  • Spend at least 15 minutes sitting quietly before God without trying to accomplish anything.
  • Eat one meal slowly and gratefully.
  • Take a gentle walk and notice signs of God's goodness around you.
Breath Prayer"My rest is an act of trust."
  • 1What made it difficult for me to slow down?
  • 2When did I feel most at peace today?
Closing Prayer"Teach me to receive rest as Your gift."

Thank God for one specific moment of grace during the day.

2Day

Letting Go of Hurry

Psalm 131
  • Avoid rushing into your Sabbath.
  • Wake up earlier than necessary so you can begin slowly.
  • Prepare a calm environment with minimal noise and distraction.
Opening Question"What is driving my hurry?"
  • Read Psalm 131 slowly.
  • Walk slower than usual throughout the day.
  • Choose one ordinary activity and do it unhurriedly.
  • Avoid multitasking.
  • Listen to worship music without doing anything else.
Breath Prayer"I am here with God."
  • 1What happens inside me when I slow down?
  • 2What fears surface when I stop hurrying?
Closing PracticeWrite down one thing you want to release to God this week. End the day with gratitude.

3Day

Turning Off the Noise

1 Kings 19:11–13
  • Reduce media consumption several hours before your Sabbath.
  • Turn off unnecessary alerts and notifications.
  • Create a quiet physical space where you can pray or reflect.
Opening Prayer"Lord, quiet the noise within me."
  • Read 1 Kings 19:11–13 slowly (the still, small voice).
  • Spend time in silence for at least 10–15 minutes.
  • Resist the urge to constantly check your phone.
  • Sit outdoors if possible and simply listen.
  • Notice how often your mind seeks distraction.
  • Gently return your attention to God's presence.
  • 1What kinds of noise most affect my soul?
  • 2Did silence feel comforting or uncomfortable today?
Closing PrayerAsk God to help you become more attentive throughout the week. Pray slowly before sleeping.

4Day

Rest Without Guilt

Exodus 20:8–11
  • Notice any feelings of guilt connected to rest.
  • Write down unfinished tasks that are tempting you mentally.
  • Offer those responsibilities to God in prayer.
Anchor Truth"God is at work even when I rest."
  • Read Exodus 20:8–11 slowly.
  • Give yourself permission to enjoy simple pleasures.
  • Take a nap if your body needs it.
  • Avoid evaluating your productivity today.
  • Spend time with someone you enjoy without pressure or agenda.
  • Receive joy without needing to "earn" it.
  • 1Why do I sometimes feel guilty when resting?
  • 2What helped me experience freedom today?
Closing PrayerThank God for caring about your well-being. End with a short prayer of surrender.

5Day

Trusting God with Unfinished Work

Psalm 127
  • List the unfinished tasks or worries occupying your mind.
  • Pray over each one slowly.
  • Put away your work materials intentionally.
Opening Prayer"I trust You with what I cannot complete today."
  • Read Psalm 127 slowly.
  • Whenever anxious thoughts about work arise, pause and breathe deeply.
  • Spend time doing something life-giving rather than productive.
  • Reflect on how God sustains the world without your constant effort.
  • Enjoy moments of delight without needing outcomes.
  • 1What unfinished work was hardest to release?
  • 2What did today teach me about trust?
Closing Prayer"My life is held securely in Your hands, O God." Ask God for wisdom and peace for the coming week.
